Pauline Chalamet Biography

Pauline Chalamet was born on January 25, 1992, in the United States to parents Nicole Flender and Marc Chalamet. She’s an actress, screenwriter, and director who acted in short films like Between Fear and Laughter and TV shows like Royal Pains.

Many people may know her as the older sister of the handsome Oscar-nominated actor Timothee Chalamet. Pauline played the role of a ballerina on Royal Pains, starring as Jill Flint. She started acting as a child, with her first debut on an episode of the soap opera One Life to Live in 1999.

She is one of the most successful Movie Actress, and she is ranked on the list of famous people who were born on January 25, 1992. She is one of the Richest Movie Actresses born in the United States.

Pauline is bilingual in English and French, and as a kid, she took piano lessons and continued in that until she graduated high school. In 2001, she began taking classes at the School of American Ballet.

Then, at age 10, she danced in a Broadway production of A Midsummer Night’s Dream. She also attended the Rosella Hightower dance school, located in Mougins, South of France.

Pauline also attended the School of American Ballet until 2010. That year, a biking accident hindered any chances she had of furthering her dancing career.

Although ambivalent about a career in acting, Pauline recalls seeing Liev Schreiber act on Talk Radio in 2006, and she says that was a pivotal moment in her decision to become an actor.

She rejected the idea of going to college at first but later applied to Bard College, where she double majored in theater and political science. She finally graduated in 2014.

While studying, Pauline worked at the school library and helped at a farm to pay off her student loans. She interned at the International Crisis Group and, for a while, dreamed of becoming a human rights lawyer. She swiftly discarded the idea.

After college, she worked odd jobs, including bartending, copyediting, and babysitting, while she continued to write in her spare time. Without her family’s knowledge, Pauline moved to Paris.

She was accepted for an acting apprenticeship at the Studio Théâtre d’Asnières in 2016. There, she fell more in love with acting.
