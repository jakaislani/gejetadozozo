Kathy Griffin Net Worth

July 2024 · 6 minute read

Net Worth and Salary

Kathy Griffin, a comedian and actress, has an estimated net worth of $35 million as of 2023. She earned her wealth through her successful career as a stand-up comedian, actress, author, and television host.

Griffin’s income comes from different sources. One of her highest-paid works was her reality show “My Life on the D-List,” which earned her $75,000 per episode. She also received $150,000 for each performance during her tour, “Kathy Griffin Live,” and $50,000 for each episode of “Kathy Griffin: My Life on the Bravo” network.

Early Life and Education

Kathleen Mary Griffin or Kathy Griffin was born on November 4, 1960, in Chicago, Illinois. She grew up in the Forest Park suburb and attended St. Bernadine’s Elementary School, Oak Park, Illinois.

At the age of 18, Griffin moved to California to pursue her acting career. However, she struggled to get roles and landed a job as a waitress in a comedy club.

Career

Griffin began her career as a stand-up comedian in the 1980s. Her breakthrough came in 1996 when she appeared on the famous TV sitcom “Seinfeld.”

In 2005, Griffin premiered her Emmy-winning reality show “My Life on the D-List.” It aired for six seasons and documented her life as a C-list celebrity.

She also hosted different shows such as “The Kathy Griffin Experience,” “Kathy Griffin: My Life on the Bravo,” and “Kathy Griffin’s Celebrity Run-Ins.”

Griffin appeared in several films such as “Pulp Fiction,” “Four Rooms,” “It’s Pat,” and “Suddenly Susan.” She also voiced characters in animated films like “Shrek Forever After” and “The Muppets.”

Personal Life

Griffin has never been married but dated tech entrepreneur Randy Bick for over eight years before they split in 2020. She is known for her LGBTQ activism and is an outspoken supporter of the community.

In July 2017, Griffin’s career took a hit when she faced a major controversy. She shared a photo of herself holding a fake bloody head resembling that of former US President, Donald Trump, which sparked outrage. She was fired from CNN’s New Year’s Eve broadcast and her comedy tour was canceled.

Real Estate

Kathy Griffin has owned various properties, including a two-story house in Hollywood Hills, which she purchased in 2004 for $2.85 million. She later sold the house in 2018 for $4.5 million.

She also bought a house in Bel Air, California, for $10.5 million but had to sell it at a loss of $2 million after the Trump controversy scandal.

Legacy

Kathy Griffin’s legacy is centered on her groundbreaking comedy career and her contribution to the LGBTQ community. She remains an advocate for marginalized communities and continues to push for diversity and inclusivity in the entertainment industry.
